Context: US new-home market, 1999-2009.
The chart shows median and average sales prices of new homes sold in the United States (monthly, US Census Bureau data).
In March 2007 the subprime mortgage industry collapsed due to higher-than-expected home foreclosure rates.
Median new-home prices peaked in early 2007 and then fell sharply through 2008-2009, the steepest decline since the 1989 Savings and Loan crisis.
If the chart shows prices dropping after 2007, the likely cause is the subprime mortgage crisis and falling demand, not construction costs.
